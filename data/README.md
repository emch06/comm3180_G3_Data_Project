## Data files for COMM3180 Group Project

* This folder contains all the data files used in our analysis of UPenn and UUIC schools.

* Raw folder serves as the raw data we gathered in the first step of our project progress. Within the raw folder, it contains Overall_Data and Business Schools folders. 

* Overall_Data contains an overall.csv file gathered from FAFSA containing Ivy League Schools and Missouri Valley Conference Schools. The csv file contains data for both UPenn and UUIC. It provides the values for earnings 10 years and 6 years pos-graduation, median debt at graduation, and the annual cost for each school. The folder also contains a sentiment analysis. In the upenn_uiuc_sentiment_sources.csv file, it describes the sources and the process (categories) gathered to create a sentiment score from -2 (negative) to 2 (positive). The upenn_uiuc_sentiment_summary contains the gathered values to create a total avg_manual_sentiment that measures the total impression towards each school.

* Within the Business Schools folder, the manual dataset labeled wharton_salaries.csv gathers the median and mean salaries for the most popular majors (accounting, finance, and marketing) within the Wharton business school in the recent years of 2021 to 2025. It also contains dataset for gies salaries (UUIC's business school), gathering the mean_salary for the accounting, finance, and marketing majors. I have also added several additional datasets which I manually created .csv files for on Excel. The first being salary_progressions which includes the standardized income trajectory of big 4 accounting firms and investment banking firms (sources are from CaseBasix and Mergers & Inquisitions). This is a standardized, albeit assumption-based, income trajectory for Gies and Wharton graduates (where accounting is the top placement in Gies and IB is the top placement in Wharton). I also looked at school costs to figure out ROI, as well as average expected tuition based on household incomes (using the US Department of Education's College Scorecards). Finally, I looked at overall industry placements to reveal what the overall career sentiments are in each college (using career outcome data from both school's career service page).
