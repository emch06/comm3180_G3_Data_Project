**⚠ CRITICAL METHODOLOGY NOTE:** Penn uses *median*; UIUC reports *average*. The mean/median gap for Grainger is large ($96,766 avg vs. $85,000 median), indicating a right-skewed distribution — a small number of high-earning CS/quant grads pull the average up significantly. When comparing, always use Penn median ($122,500) vs. UIUC median ($85,000) for an apples-to-apples comparison. The $37,500 median gap is more meaningful than the $25,600 average gap.

**⚠ KNOWLEDGE RATE NOTE:** Penn SEAS 2024 had only 98 salary respondents (n is low; coincides with large avg jump to $138K — likely selection bias toward high earners). UIUC Grainger salary data comes from 318 of 1,971 graduates = 16% salary response rate (vs. 64% overall knowledge rate via NSC/LinkedIn/social media).

## SECTION 4 — QUALITATIVE: SENTIMENT & HIREABILITY

### 4.1 Penn Alumni Survey (AY 2017, Classes of 2005–07, n=1,434, All Penn Schools)

Note: Engineering was ~12–13% of respondents (173/1,434). Wharton was 16%. Data is all-Penn, not SEAS-specific.

**Overall Satisfaction:**
- Overall satisfaction with UG education: Mean 4.49/5.0 (93% satisfied/very satisfied) vs. SPRI 4.47
- Would recommend Penn to HS senior: Mean 4.63/5.0 (74% "definitely yes") vs. SPRI 4.44 — Penn notably above peers

**Career Preparedness:**
- Prepared for current career: 4.26/5.0 vs. SPRI 4.14 — Penn outperforms
- Quantitative tools: 3.86 vs. SPRI 3.75 — Penn outperforms notably
- Being active community member: 3.58 vs. SPRI 3.91 — Penn underperforms notably
- Work/life balance: 3.93 vs. SPRI 3.96 — slightly below

**Community Perception:**
- "Very Competitive": 69% said "very"; Mean 3.65/4.0 (highest-rated community attribute)
- "Welcoming": Mean 3.40/4.0

**Values (Penn Alumni):**
- "Being well off financially" rated essential/very important: 82% vs. SPRI 70%
- "Helping others" essential: 23% vs. SPRI 29%

**Financial Outcomes (~10yr out):**
- Individual earnings mean: $124,785 vs. SPRI $110,051
- Household income mean: $182,792 vs. SPRI $170,463
- Standard of living satisfaction: 4.11/5.0 vs. SPRI 3.91 — Penn significantly outperforms
- Industry: Finance/Insurance/RE #1 (257/1,285); Healthcare #2 (230); Professional/Business Services #3 (215)

## SECTION 5 — DATA QUALITY & METHODOLOGY NOTES

| Issue | Penn SEAS | UIUC Grainger | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salary survey response rate | 46–54% direct; 74–88% knowledge rate | 318/1,971 = 16% salary respondents | UIUC salary far less representative — treat as directional |
| Overall knowledge rate | 78–88% | 64% (via survey + NSC + LinkedIn + social media) | Penn data more reliable for outcomes analysis |
| Salary measurement | Median of employed grads, first 6 months | Average (outlier-adjusted); signing bonuses excluded | Penn uses median (more robust); UIUC uses average — compare Penn median to UIUC median ($85K) not avg ($96.8K) |
| Mean vs. median gap (UIUC) | — | $85K median vs. $96.8K average = +$11.8K gap | Right-skewed; CS/quant grads pulling mean up |
| School-level breakdown | SEAS-specific static PDFs 2020–2024 | Interactive portal only for most major-level data | Penn more accessible for this analysis |
| Scale | ~450–490 SEAS grads/yr | ~1,971 Grainger grads/yr (~4x larger) | Penn smaller + more selective; UIUC broader talent pool |
| Dual degree inclusion | Separate SEAS-only vs. SEAS/WH tables | Not separated | Use SEAS-only for cleanest comp to UIUC single-degree |
| Bonus/total comp | Base salary only | Base salary only; signing bonuses tracked separately | Neither captures TC; gap likely understates Penn advantage in finance/quant roles where bonuses are large |
