- Overall: UPenn outperforms on financial outcomes (better debt-to-income ratio), yet its community expresses more
negative sentiment. Meanwhile, UIUC has a heavier debt burden but a more positive community outlook.

- Business: The findings show that Wharton consistently reports higher starting salaries across all majors, but the trends reveal more
nuance. In accounting, Gies salaries are rising while Wharton’s appear to be declining slightly, suggesting Gies’ strong
reputation in the field may be closing the gap. In finance, Wharton salaries have plateaued around ~$110K, while Gies is
gradually. In marketing, both schools show upward trends, reflecting broader industry demand.

- The findings also suggest that Wharton graduates (particularly those entering investment banking) achieve ROI faster
(around ~4 years post-grad) and reach higher long-term earnings compared to Gies accounting graduates. 

-Engineering: Penn SEAS sends ~75% directly into full-time work. UIUC Grainger routes 42% into grad school — delaying labor-market entry and ompressing near-term ROI

- Penn SEAS wins on salary: Median gap of $37,500 is earned back in under a year relative to UIUC's in-state net cost.
- UIUC leads in pure engineering
- Different outcome profiles: Penn SEAS feeds finance, consulting & tech. UIUC is a traditional engineering pipeline. Same degree — different destination.
