## Blog post an of your data story and creative output for COMM318 Group Data Project

Our project began with a viral moment during the 2026 NCAA Tournament, when the University of Pennsylvania Band directed the chant “That’s alright, that’s okay, you’re gonna work for us someday” toward University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign following Penn’s loss to UIUC. What initially appeared to be a simple case of poor sportsmanship quickly evolved into a larger national debate surrounding elitism, prestige, and whether Ivy League institutions actually produce stronger long-term outcomes than elite public universities. Using this controversy as a case study, our project investigates the broader question of how institutional prestige translates into measurable post-graduate success—and whether financial outcomes alone should define the value of a university education.

Through a mixed-methods approach combining quantitative and qualitative analysis, we compare Penn and UIUC across metrics including median earnings, return on investment (ROI), student debt, economic mobility, salary progression, career pipelines, engineering and business school outcomes, alumni geography, and institutional demographics. We additionally conducted sentiment analysis using Reddit discussions, student newspapers, alumni surveys, and online discourse to evaluate perceptions of stress, belonging, happiness, and overall undergraduate experience. Our findings reveal a far more nuanced reality than the original chant implied: Penn demonstrates substantially stronger short-term financial outcomes and elite-industry placement pipelines, while UIUC emerges as a stronger driver of broad economic mobility, community satisfaction, and student quality of life. Ultimately, this project argues that the debate between elite private and flagship public universities is not simply about which institution is “better,” but rather about which forms of success society chooses to prioritize.

Our creative output is a revised version of the chant that is actually reflective of our data findings.
